# Digital Readiness in India 🌐 | UI/UX & Product Design Case Study

### 📌 Project Overview
Developed during my research internship at **NITI Aayog**, this project focuses on designing an executive dashboard to solve the **'Granularity Gap'** in India's telecom data. While national internet penetration metrics look promising on paper, they often obscure deep regional and rural-urban disparities. 

This product translates complex infrastructure metrics into intuitive, actionable, and spatial data visualisations to help government policymakers drive targeted digital interventions.

## 🛠️ Design Architecture & Core Interfaces

The product framework is structured across three strategic views to optimize executive decision-making:

### 1. National Dashboard Overview 📊
* **Objective:** Provides a macro-level overview of country-wide data.
* **Key Features:** Tracks critical KPIs like Tele-density, subscriber growth, and the urban-rural digital divide. It gives a bird's-eye view of India's current digital health at a single glance.

### 2. Regional Insights Map 🗺️
* **Objective:** Visualises geographic data density to bridge localized divides.
* **Key Features:** Highlights 'Aspirational Districts' and remote areas where infrastructure deployment is lagging. It maps active subscriber clusters dynamically.

### 3. Gap Analysis & Benchmarking Tool 📈
* **Objective:** Bivariate analysis to isolate the core root causes of low digital adoption.
* **Key Features:** Compares infrastructure availability against actual usage. This helps officials differentiate between areas lacking physical infrastructure versus areas suffering from low digital literacy or economic barriers.

---

## 💡 Key Product Insights & Learnings

* **Information Architecture over Complexity:** Government platforms deal with massive data density. During initial user testing iterations, complex multivariate graphs led to cognitive overload. 
* **The Power of Minimalism:** Redesigned the product to adopt a clean, minimal, and progressive disclosure model—ensuring decision-makers see high-level insights first and drill down into details only when required.
